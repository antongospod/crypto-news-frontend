**Solana's** decentralized finance ecosystem has crossed the **$9 billion** total value locked (TVL) threshold for the first time, marking a significant milestone in the blockchain's recovery from the FTX collapse aftermath. The surge represents a **340% increase** from its post-FTX low of $2.1 billion in November 2022.

## Record-Breaking Growth

According to DefiLlama data, Solana's TVL reached $9.02 billion as of January 2025, driven primarily by explosive growth in liquid staking protocols and emerging real-world asset (RWA) tokenization platforms. The ecosystem's rapid expansion has positioned it as the **fourth-largest DeFi blockchain** by TVL, trailing only Ethereum, Tron, and Base.

## Leading Protocols Drive Momentum

**Jito**, Solana's leading liquid staking protocol, commands the largest share with $2.8 billion in TVL, representing nearly 31% of the ecosystem's total locked value. The protocol's native JTO token has gained 45% over the past month as institutional adoption accelerates.

Other major contributors include:
- **Raydium** (AMM): $1.6 billion TVL
- **Jupiter** (perpetual exchange): $890 million TVL
- **Marinade Finance** (liquid staking): $780 million TVL

## RWA Sector Expansion

The growth trajectory has been particularly pronounced in the **RWA sector**, where protocols like Maple Finance and Credix have collectively attracted over $400 million in institutional capital. These platforms are tokenizing corporate loans and trade finance, capitalizing on Solana's low transaction costs and high throughput capabilities.
